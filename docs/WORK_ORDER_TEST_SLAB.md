# AEROCEMENT TEST SLAB WORK ORDER (Phase 0)
**Location:** Cousin's Property (Mobile Site)
**Goal:** Validate Porosity & Curing Strength
**Scale:** 1m² Surface Area | 5cm Depth | ~25 Liters Volume

## 1. MATERIAL QUANTITIES (Single Batch)
| Material | Quantity | Source/Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Portland Cement** | 6 kg (~13 lbs) | ~1/5th of a standard 25kg bag |
| **Sand (Fine)** | 12 kg (~26 lbs) | Construction sand (screened) |
| **Activated Carbon** | 0.5 kg (~1 lb) | Filter carbon or crushed charcoal (powdered) |
| **Water** | ~4 - 5 Liters | Adjust for consistency |
| **Xanthan Gum** | 10 g (~2 tbsp) | Thickener/stabilizer |
| **Dawn Dish Soap** | 30 mL (~2 tbsp) | Surfactant (creates bubbles) |

## 2. MIXING SEQUENCE (CRITICAL)
1. **Dry Mix First:** Blend Cement + Sand + Carbon thoroughly in a wheelbarrow or bucket.
   *Check:* Color should be uniform dark gray/black.
2. **Foam Solution:** In a separate container, mix Water + Xanthan Gum. Let sit 2 mins until gelatinous.
   Add Dawn Soap gently. Do not whip yet.
3. **Combine:** Pour Foam Solution into Dry Mix.
4. **Aerate:** Mix vigorously by hand/mixer until bubbles form and volume expands (like pancake batter).
   *Target Consistency:* Thick foam, holds shape when scooped, not runny.

## 3. FORMWORK (Trash-to-Treasure)
- **Base:** Cardboard box cut to 1m x 1m. Tape seams with duct tape.
- **Lining:** Line inside with plastic sheet (trash bag) to prevent sticking.
- **Pour:** Spread mix to 5cm depth. Tap sides to settle air pockets.
- **Finish:** Smooth top with trowel.

## 4. THE 21-DAY WET CURE PROTOCOL
**WARNING:** If this dries out before day 7, strength is lost.
- **Day 1-3:** Keep covered with wet burlap/plastic immediately after pouring. Mist with water 2x daily.
- **Day 4-14:** Keep under plastic sheeting. Mist once daily.
- **Day 15-21:** Allow to dry naturally in shade.
- **Test:** On Day 21, strike with hammer. It should chip like stone, not crumble like sand.

## 5. DATA LOGGING
- Measure Temp at surface vs. ambient every 6 hours for first 48 hours (Exothermic reaction check).
- Record visual changes (cracking, color shift).
